Abstract

[cite_start]Robotic suturing is a prototypical long-horizon dexterous manipulation task, requiring coordinated needle grasping, precise tissue penetration, and secure knot tying[cite: 42].

[cite_start]Despite numerous efforts toward end-to-end autonomy, a fully autonomous suturing pipeline has yet to be demonstrated on physical hardware[cite: 43]. [cite_start]We introduce SutureBot: an autonomous suturing benchmark on the da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK), spanning needle pickup, tissue insertion, and knot tying[cite: 44]. [cite_start]To ensure repeatability, we release a high-fidelity dataset comprising 1,890 suturing demonstrations[cite: 45].

[cite_start]Furthermore, we propose a goal-conditioned framework that explicitly optimizes insertion-point precision, improving targeting accuracy by 59%-74% over a task-only baseline[cite: 46]. [cite_start]To establish this task as a benchmark for dexterous imitation learning, we evaluate state-of-the-art vision-language-action (VLA) models, including Ï€-O, GR00T N1, OpenVLA-OFT, and multitask ACT, each augmented with a high-level task-prediction policy[cite: 47]. [cite_start]Autonomous suturing is a key milestone toward achieving robotic autonomy in surgery[cite: 48]. [cite_start]These contributions support reproducible evaluation and development of precision-focused, long-horizon dexterous manipulation policies necessary for end-to-end suturing[cite: 49].

Experimental Setup

[cite_start]Our data-collection setup uses the da Vinci Research Kit (dVRK) Si version[cite: 153, 157]. [cite_start]We use a Soft Tissue Suture Pad as the task surface[cite: 158]. [cite_start]The setup includes wrist cameras, an endoscope, robot grippers, and a suture needle on the pad[cite: 130]. [cite_start]Data was collected on wound one, while wounds two through six are reserved for generalization testing[cite: 132].

Suturing Tasks

[cite_start]We decompose the suturing procedure into three distinct tasks: needle pickup, needle throw, and knot tie[cite: 176, 186]. [cite_start]These discretizations were used for data collection, policy training, and evaluation[cite: 187].
